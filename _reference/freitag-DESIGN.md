# Freitag — Anchor Reference (Refero)

> **Role for Bighorn:** the catalog / product-merchandising tier. How a utilitarian
> product company structures a warehouse-catalog storefront without looking cheap.

**Source:** https://freitag.ch · Refero style `4b1b9e1c-9186-4039-bd83-0259844fed14`
**Theme:** light · **Mood:** "part warehouse catalog, part contemporary editorial storefront"

## Why it's an anchor
Freitag sells rugged, utilitarian physical goods and makes them feel *quietly premium* —
not with gloss, but with discipline: a light gray "recycled canvas" ground, stark black
type, hairline rules, and oversized authentic product photography doing all the color
work. This is the antidote to Bighorn's flat-navy SaaS look.

## Key tokens
- **Canvas:** Silver Foam `#cacaca` page ground · Canvas White `#ffffff` panels · Light Gray `#f1f1f1` secondary
- **Ink:** pure black `#000000` text/borders/icons · Muted Ash `#616161` secondary text
- **Accent:** chromatic color comes from the *product photography itself*, not UI chrome
- **Type:** condensed heading face (AkkStdRg-like), compact uppercase utility labels, sans body
- **Radius:** cards `0px` · images `12px` · buttons `9999px` (pill)
- **Shadows:** essentially none — depth via 1px borders and tonal surface shifts
- **Layout:** 1200px max width, 64px section gap, 3-col product grids, split-screen photo heroes

## Do
- Light gray canvas as the page foundation; white panels float on it
- Let product photography carry all the color and energy
- Hairline black borders + flat surfaces for structure, never soft glowy shadows
- Condensed display type for impact; compact uppercase for utility labels
- Respect a contained 1200px width — disciplined, gallery-like

## Don't
- Don't use chromatic color for UI chrome (backgrounds, text) — reserve it for imagery
- Don't use soft low-contrast shadows
- Don't sprawl full-bleed everywhere — contain content
